Overview

The original Frederick Construction site did not reflect the professionalism of the company or the integrity of its work. As a portfolio, it also did not have the functionality to sell projects well. Old images, poorly structured layout, and the confounding navigation made it tough for people to get around. I saw a clear chance to redesign the site for better user experience.

With the new layout, I envisioned creating a sleek, intuitive user interface that exhibits Frederick Construction work in an excellent and confident way. The newly updated site now functions as an efficient portfolio with clean images, neat project teasers, and improved navigation. My focus was to make it easier for users to understand and trust the ability of the company, all while staying on tight schedule and within budget.

Process

Wireframes

During the wireframing process, I chose a "block" style to reflect the structured, reliable nature of the construction industry. Each section of the site was designed as a distinct block, making the layout easy to navigate and visually consistent. This approach helped create a strong foundation for both usability and design clarity.

Digital Experience

Built for Browsing

The online experience was optimized for a seamless and intuitive experience across all devices. I focused on performance, responsiveness, and clarity so users could easily navigate projects and services. Every interaction was made to be usable, creating a smooth experience that embodies the user-friendliness and attention of the work that goes into the construction itself.

Web Design

Structured Aesthetic

The web design follows a clean, grid-based layout that reflects the order and precision of construction work. I used a neutral color palette, bold typography, and strong visual hierarchy to highlight key content and guide users naturally through the site. The result is a polished, professional look that builds trust at first glance.
