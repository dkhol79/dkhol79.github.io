Overview

This project is inspired by the Sinosphere and the world’s forgetfulness about Vietnam. The Sinosphere includes 4 countries, China, Japan, Korea and Vietnam, sharing the East Asian cutural sphere. After many wars and the effects of globalization, Vietnam has taken a step back to deal with potential problems and almost disappeared from the world’s news. Meanwhile, the cultures of China, Japan and Korea are widely known, from food, art, music to manners, traditions and folk tales. And Vietnam was pushed into the group of Southeast Asian countries. Raya and the Last Dragon is the most recent example of this recognition of the pop culture, when the Vietnamese elements are almost invisible that the movie claimed to portray.

Market Research

A study was carried out to examine cooking trends and people's experiences with online recipes in 2022. Here are some of the findings:

Shift Towards Healthier Options

The research indicated a noticeable trend towards healthier cooking choices among consumers. This included increased interest in plant-based recipes, low-carb options, and recipes focusing on fresh, whole ingredients.

Diverse Cultural Influences

People showed a growing inclination towards exploring recipes from diverse cultures. There was a surge in interest in international cuisines, with many individuals actively seeking out recipes from various parts of the world.

Convenience Factor

Convenience remained a significant factor influencing cooking habits. Online recipes offering quick and easy meal solutions gained popularity, particularly among busy individuals seeking hassle-free cooking experiences.

Visual Appeal Matters

The study highlighted the importance of visually appealing content in online recipes. Participants expressed a preference for recipes accompanied by high-quality images or videos, as these helped in understanding the cooking process better.

Personalization and Customization

There was a growing demand for personalized and customizable recipes. Consumers appreciated platforms that allowed them to tailor recipes according to dietary preferences, allergies, or ingredient availability.

Trust and Reliability

Trustworthiness and reliability were crucial factors when selecting online recipes. Users favored platforms with credible sources, such as reputable chefs, food bloggers, or well-known cooking websites.

Social Sharing and Community Engagement

Social sharing and community engagement played a significant role in the online cooking experience. Participants valued platforms that facilitated recipe sharing, user reviews, and interactions with other cooking enthusiasts.

Competitive Research

I conducted a competitive analysis of cooking apps available on the market at the moment. My goal was to identify the strengths and weaknesses of each app based on user feedback. Here are some key insights:

Cooking with Apps

Among the popular cooking apps examined, such as "Tasty", "Yummly", and "Allrecipes", user satisfaction varied widely. "Tasty" stood out for its engaging video content, with 85% of users praising its visually appealing recipes. However, some users reported difficulty in navigating the app's interface, particularly when searching for specific recipes.

Recipe Variety and Accessibility

"Yummly" garnered positive feedback for its extensive recipe database, catering to various dietary preferences and skill levels. 70% of users appreciated the app's personalized recipe recommendations. Nevertheless, a notable drawback was encountered in terms of accessibility, as 45% of users found it challenging to adjust serving sizes or ingredient quantities.

Community Engagement and Social Features

"Allrecipes" scored well in terms of community engagement, with 60% of users enjoying the ability to interact with fellow cooks through comments and reviews. However, some users expressed frustration with the app's advertisement-heavy interface, which detracted from the overall user experience.

User-Friendly Interface and Navigation

"Kitchen Stories" received accolades for its user-friendly interface and intuitive navigation, with 80% of users commending its ease of use. Despite this, concerns were raised regarding the limited recipe selection compared to other apps, indicating room for improvement in content diversity.

Premium Features and Subscription Models

Several apps, including "Food Network Kitchen", offered premium features through subscription models. While 50% of users appreciated the additional content and features provided, others felt deterred by the high subscription costs, suggesting a need for more flexible pricing options.

Digital Experience

Interactive Elements

The digital experience was designed to engage users through interactive elements that highlight A21's mission. Animated infographics and scroll-triggered transitions create an immersive journey, while intuitive navigation ensures accessibility for all users.

Web Design

Visual Identity

The web design focused on creating a cohesive visual identity that reflects A21's mission. A restrained color palette, paired with tactile textures and bold typography, establishes a serious yet approachable tone, encouraging user engagement and trust.
